# Sociology (SOCI)
**SOCI 1301** **Principles of Sociology** **Credits:** 3 (3-0-0)
This course is designed to introduce to students to the scientific study of human interaction, social institutions, and dynamic changes in society. The focus is on the critical interplay between the individual and society, in particular, how and individual impacts society, and in turn how societal structures organize human life. This course meets the standards for the "Social and Behavioral Science" category of courses under the core curriculum. Required for all sociology majors, minors and BAAS degrees with a concentration in sociology. TSI Restriction(s): Reading, Math, and Writing
**Restrictions:** Graduate level students may not enroll.
**SOCI 1306** **Social Problems** **Credits:** 3 (3-0-0)
Application of sociological principles and theoretical perspectives to major social problems in contemporary society such as inequality, crime and violence, substance abuse, environmental issues, deviance, globalization, or family problems. This course meets the standards for the "Social and Behavioral Sciences" category of courses under the core curriculum. Required for all sociology majors, minors and for BAAS degrees with a concentration in sociology. This class is a prerequisite for [SOCI 2330](https://catalog.tamusa.edu/search/?P=SOCI%202330 "SOCI 2330") and is a prerequisite option for many sociology electives. TSI Restriction(s): Reading, Math, and Writing
**Restrictions:** Graduate level students may not enroll.
**SOCI 2330** **The Craft of Sociology** **Credits:** 3 (3-0-0)
This course prepares students for academic success as sociologists. The focus of the course is on sociological approaches to researching, reading, thinking, and writing. This course addresses key concepts in the discipline such as social structure, agency, application of the sociological imagination, and social constructionism. Required for all sociology majors, minors, and BAAS degrees with a concentration in sociology. 
**Prerequisites:** [ENGL 1302](https://catalog.tamusa.edu/search/?P=ENGL%201302 "ENGL 1302") and either [SOCI 1301](https://catalog.tamusa.edu/search/?P=SOCI%201301 "SOCI 1301") or [1306](https://catalog.tamusa.edu/search/?P=SOCI%201306 "SOCI 1306").

**SOCI 3312** **Health and Society** **Credits:** 3 (3-0-0)
This course surveys the social determinants and consequences of health and disease in the United States. It moves beyond a biological perspective to understand how disparities across different social statuses, such as social class, race and ethnicity, and gender, impact health outcomes. This course fulfills a requirement for the Bachelor’s of Science in Sociology degree. TSI Restriction(s): Reading, Math, and Writing

**SOCI 4363** **Afrx-Latinxs: Race, Culture, and Transnationalism** **Credits:** 3 (3-0-0)
Through lectures, readings, film analysis, discussion and writing it is the goal of this course to illuminate the processes, actions, and histories that have impacted the lives of Afrx-Latinx people. This course will illustrate how Afrx-Latinx are a complex group of people with different experiences in the United States and that are deeply embedded in the histories, locations, structures and institutions of the United States. We will also investigate the structures that organize the migration flows from the Caribbean, Central and South American Nations. TSI Restriction(s): Reading, Math, and Writing
**Prerequisites:** [SOCI 1301](https://catalog.tamusa.edu/search/?P=SOCI%201301 "SOCI 1301") or [ENGL 1302](https://catalog.tamusa.edu/search/?P=ENGL%201302 "ENGL 1302").
